About Us





Breast cancer was one of the first types of cancer heavily impacted by the identification of key biomarkers. Since the discovery of key hormone receptors such as the estrogen receptor (ER), progesterone receptor (PR), and human epidermal growth factor receptor 2 (HER2), treatments targeted to these specific biomarkers have helped hundreds of thousands of women with breast cancer live longer. Currently cancer biomarkers are a powerful tool for physicians, enabling them develop personalized breast cancer treatment for each patient based on the presence, absence, or over-expression of certain hormone receptors and biomarkers.
